Medical Terminology Basics

Language of healthcare and medicine

What is Medical Terminology?

Medical terminology is the specialized language used by healthcare professionals to describe the human body, medical conditions, procedures, and treatments. It is primarily based on Greek and Latin roots, prefixes, and suffixes, creating a standardized system for communication in the medical field.

Key Components

  • • Root Words: The core meaning of a medical term (e.g., "cardi" = heart, "derm" = skin)
  • • Prefixes: Added before the root word to modify meaning (e.g., "hyper-" = excessive, "hypo-" = deficient)
  • • Suffixes: Added after the root word to indicate condition, procedure, or disease (e.g., "-itis" = inflammation, "-ology" = study of)
  • • Combining Forms: Root words combined with vowels to create compound terms

Common Medical Terms

  1. 1. Cardiology: Study of the heart ("cardio" = heart, "-ology" = study of)
  2. 2. Dermatitis: Inflammation of the skin ("derm" = skin, "-itis" = inflammation)
  3. 3. Hypertension: High blood pressure ("hyper-" = excessive, "tension" = pressure)
  4. 4. Gastroenterology: Study of the stomach and intestines ("gastro" = stomach, "entero" = intestine)
  5. 5. Neurology: Study of the nervous system ("neuro" = nerve, "-ology" = study of)

Importance in Healthcare

Medical terminology ensures precise communication among healthcare professionals, reducing the risk of misunderstandings. It allows for accurate documentation of patient conditions, clear descriptions of procedures, and standardized medical records. Understanding medical terminology is essential for anyone working in healthcare, from doctors and nurses to medical coders and transcriptionists.
