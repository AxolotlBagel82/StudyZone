Algebra Basics

Study of mathematical symbols and rules

What is Algebra?

Algebra is a branch of mathematics that uses symbols, letters, and numbers to represent quantities and relationships. It extends arithmetic by introducing variables (unknown values) and allows us to solve equations and understand mathematical patterns.

Key Concepts

  • • Variables: Letters (like x, y, z) that represent unknown values
  • • Equations: Mathematical statements showing two expressions are equal (e.g., 2x + 3 = 11)
  • • Expressions: Combinations of numbers, variables, and operations (e.g., 3x + 5)
  • • Operations: Addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and exponentiation

Major Topics

  1. 1. Linear Equations: Equations with variables to the first power (ax + b = c)
  2. 2. Quadratic Equations: Equations with variables squared (ax² + bx + c = 0)
  3. 3. Polynomials: Expressions with multiple terms involving variables and coefficients
  4. 4. Systems of Equations: Multiple equations solved simultaneously
  5. 5. Factoring: Breaking down expressions into simpler components

Key Principles

Algebra follows fundamental rules: the order of operations (PEMDAS), properties of equality (what you do to one side, you must do to the other), and the distributive property. These principles allow us to manipulate equations and solve for unknown variables. Algebra is essential for advanced mathematics, science, engineering, and many real-world problem-solving applications.
