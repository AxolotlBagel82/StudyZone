Geometry Basics

Study of shapes, sizes, and properties of space

What is Geometry?

Geometry is the branch of mathematics that studies shapes, sizes, positions, and properties of space. It deals with points, lines, angles, surfaces, and solids, exploring their relationships and measurements.

Key Concepts

  • • Points: Locations in space with no size or dimension
  • • Lines: Straight paths extending infinitely in both directions
  • • Angles: Formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint, measured in degrees
  • • Shapes: Two-dimensional figures (triangles, circles, polygons) and three-dimensional solids (cubes, spheres, cylinders)

Major Topics

  1. 1. Plane Geometry: Two-dimensional shapes and their properties (triangles, circles, quadrilaterals)
  2. 2. Solid Geometry: Three-dimensional shapes and their volumes and surface areas
  3. 3. Coordinate Geometry: Using coordinate systems to describe geometric figures
  4. 4. Transformations: Translations, rotations, reflections, and dilations
  5. 5. Theorems: Pythagorean theorem, triangle similarity, circle properties

Key Principles

Geometry uses axioms (self-evident truths) and theorems (proven statements) to establish relationships. Key formulas include area calculations (rectangle: length × width, circle: πr²), perimeter, volume, and surface area. Geometry is essential for architecture, engineering, design, navigation, and understanding spatial relationships in the physical world.
