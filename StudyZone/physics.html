Physics Basics

Study of matter, energy, and forces

What is Physics?

Physics is the fundamental science that studies matter, energy, space, and time. It seeks to understand the natural laws that govern the universe, from subatomic particles to galaxies.

Core Concepts

  • • Mechanics: Motion, forces, and energy
  • • Thermodynamics: Heat, temperature, and energy transfer
  • • Electromagnetism: Electric and magnetic fields
  • • Waves: Oscillations and wave phenomena
  • • Quantum Mechanics: Behavior of particles at atomic scales

Major Branches

  1. 1. Classical Mechanics: Newton's laws of motion and gravity
  2. 2. Thermodynamics: Laws of energy and entropy
  3. 3. Electromagnetism: Electric charges, currents, and fields
  4. 4. Optics: Light and its interactions with matter
  5. 5. Modern Physics: Relativity and quantum mechanics

Fundamental Laws

Newton's laws describe motion and forces. Conservation laws (energy, momentum, mass) state that these quantities remain constant in closed systems. The laws of thermodynamics govern energy flow. Einstein's E=mc² relates mass and energy. Quantum mechanics explains atomic and subatomic behavior.
