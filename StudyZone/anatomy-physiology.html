Anatomy & Physiology Basics

Study of body structure and biological function

What is Anatomy & Physiology?

Anatomy is the study of the structure of the body and its parts. Physiology is the study of how those parts function and work together. Together, they provide a complete understanding of the human body.

Anatomical Concepts

  • • Body Systems: Integumentary, skeletal, muscular, nervous, endocrine, cardiovascular, lymphatic, respiratory, digestive, urinary, reproductive
  • • Anatomical Position: Standard reference position for describing body parts
  • • Body Planes: Sagittal, frontal, and transverse planes divide the body
  • • Directional Terms: Superior, inferior, anterior, posterior, medial, lateral

Physiological Systems

  1. 1. Nervous System: Controls body functions through electrical signals
  2. 2. Cardiovascular System: Circulates blood and transports nutrients
  3. 3. Respiratory System: Exchanges gases (oxygen and carbon dioxide)
  4. 4. Digestive System: Breaks down food and absorbs nutrients
  5. 5. Muscular System: Produces movement and generates heat

Key Principles

Homeostasis maintains stable internal conditions. Cells are the basic units of life. Tissues form organs, which create organ systems. The body uses feedback mechanisms (positive and negative) to regulate functions. All systems work together to maintain life.
