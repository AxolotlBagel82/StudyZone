Kinesiology Basics

Study of human movement and performance

What is Kinesiology?

Kinesiology is the scientific study of human movement, performance, and function. It combines principles from anatomy, physiology, biomechanics, and psychology to understand how the body moves and performs.

Key Concepts

  • • Biomechanics: The study of forces acting on the body and the effects they produce
  • • Motor Control: How the nervous system coordinates movement
  • • Exercise Physiology: How the body responds and adapts to physical activity
  • • Movement Analysis: Observing and analyzing human movement patterns

Major Areas of Study

  1. 1. Anatomical Kinesiology: Structure and function of bones, muscles, and joints
  2. 2. Biomechanical Kinesiology: Forces, torques, and motion in human movement
  3. 3. Physiological Kinesiology: Energy systems and metabolic responses
  4. 4. Motor Learning: How movement skills are acquired and refined

Applications

Kinesiology is applied in physical therapy, athletic training, ergonomics, rehabilitation, sports performance, and exercise prescription. Professionals use kinesiology principles to improve movement efficiency, prevent injuries, and enhance performance.
