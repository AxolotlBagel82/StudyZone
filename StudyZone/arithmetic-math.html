Arithmetic Math Basics

Study of basic number operations

What is Arithmetic?

Arithmetic is the branch of mathematics dealing with basic number operations: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. It forms the foundation for all other areas of mathematics and is used in everyday life for calculations and problem-solving.

Four Basic Operations

  • • Addition (+): Combining two or more numbers to find their sum
  • • Subtraction (-): Finding the difference between two numbers
  • • Multiplication (×): Repeated addition, finding the product of numbers
  • • Division (÷): Splitting a number into equal parts, finding quotients

Major Topics

  1. 1. Whole Numbers: Counting numbers and zero (0, 1, 2, 3...)
  2. 2. Integers: Positive and negative whole numbers
  3. 3. Fractions: Parts of a whole, represented as ratios (e.g., 1/2, 3/4)
  4. 4. Decimals: Numbers with decimal points representing fractional parts
  5. 5. Order of Operations: Rules for solving expressions (PEMDAS: Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division, Addition/Subtraction)

Key Principles

Arithmetic follows fundamental properties: commutative (order doesn't matter for addition/multiplication), associative (grouping doesn't matter), and distributive (multiplication distributes over addition). Mastery of arithmetic is essential for daily life tasks like budgeting, cooking, shopping, and forms the foundation for algebra, geometry, and advanced mathematics.
